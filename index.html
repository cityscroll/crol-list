What this is

The City Record is NYC's official daily journal: every agency must publish its procurement solicitations, award notices, public hearings, and property dispositions here (City Charter §1066). This tool reads that same data live from NYC Open Data — dataset City Record Online (dg92-zbpx), the structured form of the printed paper — and re-links notices that share a Procurement Identification Number (PIN).

Honest caveats (the data is messy)

• Junk PINs. Some notices carry placeholder PINs (NoPINFound, 000000, punctuation). Those can't be linked; the tool shows the notice alone and says so.

• Blanket PINs. A few PINs (e.g. emergency declarations) bundle many unrelated awards under one code. The chain then shows them all — that is the real data, not a bug.

• Dollar outliers. Raw contract_amount contains erroneous mega-values; agency totals exclude rows above $5B to keep the headline honest. Confirm any figure against the official City Record and Checkbook NYC.

Sources: City Record Online (CROL) · NYC Open Data dg92-zbpx · Checkbook NYC. Data fetched live; figures as published by the City.
